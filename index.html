The Attic Rain Problem

What is attic rain?

If you've noticed ceiling stains, frost in the attic, or moisture during a Chinook — that's attic rain. Here's what's happening and why Calgary is uniquely affected.

Attic rain is moisture that condenses and freezes inside your attic over a Calgary winter, then melts rapidly during a warm spell — dripping onto insulation, drywall, and ceilings.

It's not a roof leak. The shingles are fine. The problem happens under the roof, where warm household air meets freezing attic surfaces.

Calgary's Chinooks swing temperatures 30°C in hours. That fast swing turns built-up frost into sudden "rain" inside the attic. Few climates on earth do this as often.

A 30°C swing in a single day is normal here. That's the whole problem.

The Fix

Three things stop attic rain

Done together, these three steps prevent frost from forming in the first place.

Ventilation

Balanced intake-and-exhaust venting keeps the attic cold and dry so frost can't build up.

Bath-fan adapter

A sealed adapter routes moist bathroom air fully outside instead of dumping it into the attic.

Insulation

Correct depth and R-value keeps house heat out of the attic, the root cause of melt.
